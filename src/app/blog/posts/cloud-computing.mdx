## What Is Cloud Computing?

At its core, **cloud computing** is the delivery of computing services ‚Äî like servers, storage, databases, networking, software ‚Äî over the internet.

Instead of buying and managing physical hardware, you rent what you need from providers like AWS, Google Cloud, or Microsoft Azure.

This turns IT infrastructure into a utility: **pay as you go, scale when you need, no hardware headaches.**

---

## The 3 Main Types of Cloud Services

### 1. Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)

- Virtual machines, storage, networks
- You manage the OS and apps
- Examples: Amazon EC2, Google Compute Engine

### 2. Platform as a Service (PaaS)

- A platform to build and deploy apps without managing infrastructure
- Handles scaling, patching, provisioning
- Examples: Heroku, Google App Engine, Vercel

### 3. Software as a Service (SaaS)

- Fully managed apps accessed via the web
- Just use it ‚Äî no setup or maintenance
- Examples: Gmail, Slack, Notion, Figma

## Challenges to Consider

- **Cost management**: Easy to overspend without tracking
- **Vendor lock-in**: Migrating away can be hard
- **Security**: Misconfigurations can expose sensitive data
- **Latency**: Cloud isn't always faster for real-time apps
